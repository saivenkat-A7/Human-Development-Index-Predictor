About This Project

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a composite statistic of life expectancy, education, and per-capita income indicators, used by the UNDP to rank countries into four tiers of human development: Very High, High, Medium, and Low.

Indicators Used
  • Life Expectancy at Birth — health dimension
  • Mean Years of Schooling & Expected Years of Schooling — education dimension
  • GNI per Capita (PPP $) — standard of living dimension
How Predictions Are Made

Two models are trained on a labeled dataset built from the official UNDP HDI formula (geometric mean of the three normalized dimension indices):

  • A Random Forest Classifier predicts the HDI tier (Very High / High / Medium / Low).
  • A Random Forest Regressor predicts the continuous HDI score (0–1).

For transparency, every prediction is shown alongside the exact official formula result, so you can compare the ML model's output to the ground-truth calculation.

Tech Stack

Python, Flask, scikit-learn, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Bootstrap 5.
